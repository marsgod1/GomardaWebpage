Gomarda Wildlife Sanctuary

About the Sanctuary

Gomarda wildlife sanctuary stretched across 277.82 sqkm is 52 kilometer away from Raigarh and is named after the village Gomarda situated inside the sanctuary. Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is one of the wealthiest sanctuary in terms of biodiversity and is also very well endowed in scenic beauty. The exquisite natural beauty of Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is the virtue of its vivid geographical landforms which consist of mountains, valleys, plateau, caves, lakes, waterfalls, plains, etc. Special contributions in the promotion of Gomarda wildlife sanctuary has been made by Raja Shri Javahir Singh and Raja Shri Naresh Chandra Singh rulers of Sarangarh Province.

Flora and fauna

Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is especially rich in biodiversity as various species of plants , birds and animals habituate the land.

  • Flora & Fauna
  • Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is especially rich in biodiversity as various species of plants , birds and animals habituate the land.
  • Fauna
  • Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is one of the best sanctuaries of Chhattishgarh in terms of animal sightings. The vast grasslands stretched across the sanctuary provide a very high visibility, making spotting of wildlife facile. The animals found in Gomarda in order of their probability of sighting are
    1. Bison 2. Spotted deer 3. Neelgai 4. Sambhar 5. Kotri 6. Four Horned Antelope 7. Sloth Bear 8. Wild Boar 9. Hare 10. Panther
  • Gomarda wildlife sanctuary is specially famous for Bison having around 400 of these. Birds found in Gomarda in order of their probability of sightings are
    1. Peacock 2. Doodhraaj 3. Robin 4. Kingfisher 5. Eagle 6. Hawks 7. Plum headed parakeets 8. Green Bee Eater 9. Wood Pecker 10. Red Jungle Fowl
  • Flora
  • Large grasslands are the key feature of Gomarda , these grasslands resemble the grasslands of Bandhavgarh National Park. Apart from having grasslands in the plain region of the sanctuary, it is also having small hill deciduous vegetation which includes trees such as Bhirra, Karra, Bija, Harra, Amla, Baheda etc.

  • Best Time to visit
  • The best time to visit the Reserve is 15 October to 15 June.

Places to see

  1. Tamtora:- In the middle of dense green forest surrounded by hills is a lake filled with blooming lotuses, petrifies its beholders through its astounding serenity. There is a forest resthouse available for the accommodation of tourists just beside the lake.

  2. Madosilli:- About 20kms away from Tamtora is the waterfall of Madosilli. Stream of water flowing through dense forest having qualities of various herbs runs through madosilli from July to January forms the perfect picnic spot.
